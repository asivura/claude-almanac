## Key Takeaways

### Main Lessons

1. **Coding is Becoming Management**: The role of a developer is shifting from manually writing syntax to managing a fleet of AI agents. The guest notes he keeps ~5 agents running simultaneously.
1. **Platform Agnosticism**: Coding is no longer bound to a desktop terminal. Significant work is now done via desktop apps and even mobile iOS apps, allowing for "coding" immediately upon waking up.
1. **Generational Skepticism is Cyclical**: Just as punch card programmers likely doubted the validity of software "coding," current developers may doubt natural language prompting. However, the field inevitably evolves toward higher abstraction.

### Actionable Advice

- [ ] **Shift Mindset to Management**: Start viewing yourself as a manager of agents rather than a sole contributor.
- [ ] **Utilize Mobile Tools**: Explore using mobile interfaces (like the Claude iOS app) to monitor and unblock agents during downtime or away from the desk.
- [ ] **Embrace Abstraction**: Don't get hung up on whether prompting is "real coding." Focus on the output and the ability to control the logic.
